ceramic exhibiton

silo park

25th-26th Apr
10am-6pm

Why

Clay keeps the brain flexible. Knowing what you can fix, and thinking outside the box to do so are very valuable skills. Life, like pottery, is a process. Before you can throw, you must wedge your clay, eliminating air bubbles and aligning the tiny platelets.

Pottery can be one’s personal meditation, a time to get lost in the feeling of your hands on the swirling clay and your feet on the ground. Dragging a carving tool over the surface of a just-dry piece or painting on glaze, unloading a kiln or recycling old clay - every part of the process was so magical. You felt stable and relaxed and utterly free of all your obligations and worries.

About

In circles is a two-day ceramic exhibition, bringing together ceramic artists, amateur and enthusiasts from around New Zealand. Learning from experienced artists and connecting with like-minded people, In circles provides a space where everyone can get what they are looking for in the ceramic industry.

This event is free entry, and welcoming all gender and age groups, payment made upon any purchase can be paid both in cash and cards. Please note, that workshops need to be booked prior to the exhibition, and a full payment must be paid for securing your reservation. Click to check out the workshop timetables and refund policy.

During this exhibition, the market place will give comers the chance to purchase beautiful ceramic pieces from artists, make friends who share the same interest and hobby as well as get your hands-on learning the basics of ceramic and get a chance to throw your own ceramic artwork.

At this event, you will have the chance to savor the most beautiful ceramic works from the most talented artists from Auckland, talk to different artists and enjoy a fun, remarkable ceramic workshop with people who share the same hobby and interest.

There also will be a market place where you can purchase the ceramic products ranging from dinner ware to every day use, from ornamental to jewellery, all handmade by the artists, and a lounge/open area will also be available upon the day, for you to relax, network and enjoy every moment of being warmed by the sun.

Location

Silo Park is situated in the Wynyard Quarter – an area of exciting revitalisation in the heart of Auckland’s waterfront. Wynyard Quarter is to the west of Viaduct Harbour (and the CBD as a whole!) and north of Victoria Park in an area that was once known to many Aucklanders’ as the Western Reclamation, or the Tank Farm.

Head towards Wynyard Quarter and you’ll find Silo Park on the corner of Jellicoe and Beaumont Street – just look out for the giant Silos!
